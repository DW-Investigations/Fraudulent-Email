# Fraudulent-Email
Imposing SARS Authority 

This repository contains a fraud examination case study focused on analyzing a suspicious email identified as potential fraud. The objective of this exercise is to demonstrate investigative thinking, fraud detection techniques, and structured reporting consistent with entry-level cybercrime and fraud examination practices.

The primary artifact in this case is the document:

**`16 April 2026 Fraudulent Email.pdf`**

---

## Case Summary

This case involves the analysis of a potentially fraudulent email designed to deceive the recipient through social engineering techniques. The investigation focuses on identifying red flags commonly associated with phishing and email-based fraud schemes.

Key areas of analysis include:

- Email header and sender analysis
- Suspicious language patterns and urgency tactics
- Domain authenticity and spoofing indicators
- Links and attachment risk assessment
- Social engineering techniques used

---

## Objectives

The goals of this case study are to:

- Identify indicators of email fraud (phishing attempt)
- Apply basic fraud examination principles
- Document findings in a structured investigative format
- Develop foundational skills in cyber fraud detection

---

## Methodology

The analysis approach includes:

1. Reviewing the email content for suspicious indicators
2. Evaluating sender identity and domain legitimacy
3. Identifying psychological manipulation tactics
4. Assessing potential risk to the recipient
5. Summarizing findings in a professional report format
